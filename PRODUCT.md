# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Portfolio reviewers, design-minded founders, and technical GTM leaders evaluating Seth's ability to turn a complex infrastructure story into a memorable web artifact. They arrive with little patience for generic SaaS visuals and need to understand, within one hero viewport, that the work combines commercial positioning, product storytelling, and polished frontend execution.

## Product Purpose

Showcase a fintech bank-connectivity landing hero in two variants: a precise dotted-world-map hero and a more cinematic 3D globe hero. Success means both routes feel like finished case-study artifacts, not archive experiments, while still sharing one coherent brand system.

## Brand Personality

Precise, infrastructural, quietly cinematic. The surface should feel like a premium banking API blueprint that learned to breathe: crisp, measured, technical, but not sterile.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI/SaaS cream pages, dark neon crypto dashboards, navy-and-gold finance cliches, over-rounded bento-card templates, decorative glassmorphism, gradient text, and hero sections that rely on a large metric template instead of a strong visual thesis.

## Design Principles

1. One viewport, one argument: Acme turns messy bank relationships into one programmable surface.
2. Infrastructure should feel physical: layers, coordinates, ledgers, routes, and signals should imply machinery under the page.
3. The two hero variants must read as siblings, not duplicated pages with a swapped asset.
4. Polish through precision: alignment, copy density, motion restraint, and tactile UI details carry the premium feel.
5. Keep portfolio clarity: a reviewer should instantly know what the hero is demonstrating and why it is worth saving.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls, preserve semantic headings and links, avoid text below 14px on mobile, keep touch targets at least 44px, and respect reduced-motion preferences for animated globe, map, route, and page-load effects.
